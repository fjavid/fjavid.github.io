Grokking on modular addition

March 2026 · github.com/fjavid/psi

I put together a small study of grokking—the delayed generalization that sometimes appears long after a model has memorized the training set. The code trains a tiny decoder-only transformer on modular addition mod 97, logs a pile of diagnostics (norms, gradients, Fourier structure in embeddings, PCA snapshots, operation tables), and ends with a train-fraction × weight-decay ablation that maps memorization, delayed generalization, and immediate generalization.

The run I care about most used 40% of the full 9,409-pair table for training, AdamW with weight decay 0.3, and 100k steps on a GPU. Train accuracy hit ~99% in a few hundred steps; test accuracy stayed low for a long plateau, then jumped to essentially perfect on the held-out pairs—classic grokking, as in the curves below. Separately, Fourier concentration in the number embeddings rises steadily across that plateau while test accuracy is flat—consistent with “gradual inside, abrupt outside.”

Details, pilot sweep, ablation plots, and a longer write-up live in github.com/fjavid/psi.
